## [Global Learning Poverty]: Data Analysis & BI Reporting 
## Overview
This project leverages IBM Cognos Analytics to transform raw data into actionable business intelligence. By utilizing advanced reporting and interactive dashboards, the project identifies key trends, optimizes data exploration, and provides a clear narrative for stakeholders.


## Objectives
**Data Modeling:** Create a robust data module to ensure accurate reporting.

**Interactive Visualization:** Design intuitive dashboards for real-time data exploration.

**Business Insights:** Extract high-impact findings to support data-driven decision-making.

**Technical Mastery:** Demonstrate proficiency in IBM Cognos tools, including calculations, filters, and conditional formatting.


## Dataset Profile 

**Name:**[Global child illitercay under 10 worldwide]

**Source:** [`data/child_illiteracy_by_country.csv`](./child_illiteracy_by_country.csv)

**Format:** CSV

**Dimensions:** [15] columns * [1105] rows

**Domain:** Education and literacy

**Description:** Global Child Illiteracy Under 10 — Worldwide Dataset is a comprehensive, research-grade dataset examining child illiteracy and learning poverty among children aged 0–10 years across 65+ countries worldwide.


## Key Insights
**Trend 1 (Regional Literacy Gaps):** Low-income regions show a learning poverty rate exceeding 70%, with a significant stagnation in literacy improvement over the last decade despite increased school enrollment.

**Anomaly 2 (The Gender Paradox):** While enrollment rates are reaching parity in many regions, female students in specific sub-Saharan clusters demonstrate higher literacy retention rates than males, despite having less access to extracurricular resources.

**Prediction 3 (SDG 4 Outlook):** Based on current trajectory modeling, without a 15% increase in foundational literacy intervention, the target to halve global learning poverty by 2030 will likely be delayed by at least 12 years.
